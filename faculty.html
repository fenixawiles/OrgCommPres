Faculty & Research Impact

How the Integration Strengthens Research, Collaboration, and Academic Careers

A Unified Research Enterprise

Prior to the integration, UT Health San Antonio and UTSA were already major research institutions. UT Health San Antonio employed roughly 8,700 faculty and staff, including about 4,000 faculty members and 1,400 healthcare providers, and generated $413 million in sponsored program awards annually. UTSA contributed an additional 7,000 employees and $152 million in annual R&D expenditures.

Post-merger, UT San Antonio reports a combined workforce of approximately 17,000 faculty and staff and more than $486 million in annual research funding, along with over 500 active clinical trials. This level of scale places the unified institution among the largest and most research-intensive academic enterprises in Texas.

Enhanced Interdisciplinary Collaboration

The UT System Integration FAQ highlights that the merger is designed to “foster more seamless collaboration among faculty in teaching, research, healthcare and service to facilitate maximum success in the classroom, lab and across the continuum of patient care.”

Faculty now have greater access to cross-disciplinary teams linking:

  • Health sciences and biomedical research
  • Engineering and applied sciences
  • Data science, AI, and machine learning
  • Clinical medicine and translational research
  • Public health, population science, and community outreach

These structural changes increase competitiveness for major federal grants and support deeper collaborations with industry, government agencies, and military partners.

Strengthened Research Competitiveness

The integration deck and UT Health San Antonio reports highlight several major research strengths that position the merged institution as a national competitor:

  • Five NIH-funded centers within UT Health San Antonio
  • A top 3% global ranking for NIH funding
  • A top 2% global ranking in clinical medicine specialties
  • UTSA’s federal-scale research assets, including:
    • CyManII, a $120 million DOE Cybersecure Manufacturing Institute
    • An NSF-funded National Hub for Neuromorphic Computing
  • R1 (very high research activity) designation

The merger connects these strengths, creating aligned research clusters instead of isolated silos and enabling Texas to compete for larger, more complex scientific initiatives.

New Research Facilities and Infrastructure

Faculty benefit directly from major new research and clinical infrastructure built around the merger. These investments expand the university’s capacity to conduct high-impact science and attract research-active faculty.

  • Science One — a $100 million facility supporting cancer, dementia, aging, and diabetes research
  • NeuroRecovery Research Lab — advancing spinal cord injury rehabilitation science
  • UT Health San Antonio Multispecialty & Research Hospital — a $471 million clinical and research facility
  • Center for Global and Community Oral Health
  • Center for Regenerative Sciences

These facilities create new opportunities for grant acquisition, lab expansion, translational research, and interdisciplinary innovation.

Faculty Roles, Governance, and Mission Alignment

UT System leadership emphasizes that the merger strengthens faculty governance and philanthropic alignment by presenting donors with unified initiatives rather than competing ones. Faculty are supported within a single, mission-driven institution that integrates health, research, teaching, commercialization, and public service.

The new mission statement approved by the Regents reinforces this direction, emphasizing excellence in:

  • Education
  • Research, discovery, and commercialization
  • Health and clinical service
  • Public service
  • Community engagement

These institutional priorities expand faculty influence beyond traditional academic work, strengthening roles in statewide health service, innovation, and economic development.

Key Takeaways for Faculty

  • The merger creates a 17,000-person academic workforce with one of the largest integrated research enterprises in Texas.
  • More than $486 million in annual research funding and 500+ clinical trials support faculty research and collaboration.
  • Faculty gain enhanced interdisciplinary opportunities across health, engineering, AI, and biomedical science.
  • New facilities and research centers significantly expand lab space and program capacity.
  • Mission alignment strengthens commercialization, public service, and research translation efforts.
